## 1. Bluetooth Vulnerabilities

### Insecure Pairing

* Weak or no authentication during pairing
* Allows unauthorized access

### Device Spoofing

* Attacker impersonates a trusted device
* Mimics device name or MAC address

### On-Path (Man-in-the-Middle) Attacks

* Attacker intercepts and modifies Bluetooth traffic
* Users are unaware of eavesdropping

### Bluejacking

## 3. Bluetooth Security Best Practices

### Disable Bluetooth When Not in Use

* Reduces attack surface

### Set Devices to Non-Discoverable

* Prevents unknown devices from seeing you

### Keep Firmware Updated

* Patches known Bluetooth vulnerabilities

### Pair Only with Trusted Devices

* Avoid unknown or public connections

### Use Strong, Unique PINs or Passkeys

* Protects pairing process

### Reject Unsolicited Requests

* Files or connection requests may contain malware

### Use Encryption for Sensitive Data

* Protects data if intercepted
