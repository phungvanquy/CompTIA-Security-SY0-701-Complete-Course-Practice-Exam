## 1. What Are Patches and Why They Matter

* Software developers release **patches** to fix bugs and security flaws.
* Patches apply to:

  * Operating systems
  * Applications
  * Firmware
  * Cloud instances
* Many real-world breaches happen simply because **systems were missing patches**.

**Key risk:**
Attackers often **reverse-engineer patches shortly after release** to discover vulnerabilities. If you delay patching, your systems become easy targets.

* Scanning systems for vulnerabilities

## 4. Elements of an Effective Patch Management Program

An organization should have:

1. **Assigned responsibility**

   * A person or team tracks vendor patch bulletins

2. **Patch deployment mechanism**

   * Preferably automated
   * Covers OS and applications

3. **Cloud patching coverage**

   * Cloud systems must be included

4. **Patch prioritization**

   * Urgent
   * Important
   * Non-critical

5. **Test/Lab environment**

   * Test urgent and important patches before production

6. **Logging and documentation**

   * Track patch deployments
   * Monitor effectiveness

7. **Firmware update process**

   * Firmware must also be evaluated and patched

8. **Emergency patch process**

   * Approved by an Emergency Change Advisory Board (ECAB)
   * Allows rapid deployment of critical hotfixes

9. **Scheduled non-critical rollouts**

   * Example: bi-weekly combined deployment
